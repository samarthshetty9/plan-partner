Why Gold is Not Being Activated for the Pilot
The Gold tier reward is set at 5,000 MHP, which requires approximately two complete 30-day cycles of strong
adherence to reach. At the pilot stage, with 15 patients and a single clinic, there are two reasons this tier is not
being prioritised right now.
First, patients physically cannot accumulate enough points in one month to reach Gold. It is a two-cycle
milestone by design, which means it is not relevant to a 30-day pilot.
Second, the reward attached to Gold is a 50% discount on a future care plan purchase. At a care plan price of
Rs. 499, a 50% discount means we collect Rs. 249.50 per Gold redemption instead of Rs. 499. With 15
patients this does not represent a direct cash outflow but it does reduce what is already a small initial revenue
base. At this stage it makes more sense to hold Gold as a welcome-back incentive that the company can
activate once the programme has proven its value and patient volumes are large enough to absorb the
discount without it affecting overall margins.
The Gold tier will remain in the system and visible to patients as a future goal. It just will not be redeemable
during the pilot.

For the teleconsultation reward, the pilot clinic is the obvious first partner. A discounted or complimentary
